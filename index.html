South China sea

Tensions between People's Republic of China and both the Philippines and Vietnam have recently calmed, even when People's Republic of China increased its military operations in the South China Sea by increasing Naval tactics on March and April 2018.

Meanwhile, People's Republic of China continues to construct military and industrial posts on artificial islands that were built in disputed waters of the South China Sea

The United States has also increased its military power and naval scene in that region in the recent years, including "freedom of navigation operations" (FONOPs in short) in January and March 2018.

In a speech during his visit to Southeast Asia in November 2017, Former President Trump pointed out the importance of these operations, and of ensuring open access to the South China Sea.

Since May 2017, the United States has conducted six FONOPs in the region.
